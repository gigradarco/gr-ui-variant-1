# UX Journey

## Concrete User Journey

### 1) Onboarding (One Time)
Sign up, pick your cities, and declare your music taste (techno, jazz, indie, etc.). Follow friends or import contacts. This builds your taste profile immediately, similar to how Strava asks what sports you do.

### 2) Friday Afternoon — The Trigger Moment
You open Buzo and see a feed.

- Your friend Marcus asked the agent, "any good techno tonight in Marina Bay."
- Sarah is planning to go to Headquarters.
- Two others from your network are interested in the same jazz night at Esplanade.

You did not have to search. The social feed handled discovery for you.

### 3) You Interact
You tap into the jazz night. You see the agent's credibility score, venue vibe, and audio preview. You hit "I'm going." Your friends get notified. Now it is a plan, not just an idea.

### 4) The Agent Kicks In
"You're going with Sarah and two others. Show starts 9PM. Recommended arrival 8:30PM. Want me to book a Grab for 8:15PM?"

This is the concierge closing the loop.

### 5) Post-Event — The Strava Moment
You log it: "Attended Esplanade Jazz Night." Your profile updates. Friends can see your gig history. Your taste profile gets smarter. Someone sees your history and follows you because your taste matches theirs.

### 6) The Viral Mechanic
Your "I'm going" is visible to your network. That single tap is the share action. No friction, no explicit sharing required. Attendance itself becomes the content, exactly like Strava's workout post.

### 7) The Compounding Effect
The more friends use it, the more signal appears in your feed and the more plans form organically. Network effects kick in. This is why Facebook Events worked: it was not the listing, it was seeing who was going.

## For Today's Pitch
You do not need to have built this yet. Show the concept as a flow in your demo script. Tell judges, "the Telegram bot is our MVP backend — here's what the full social layer looks like." This is honest and shows product vision, which is what the Unicorn prize rewards.
